9 October 2025

Meet Moe: my fluffy coding companion

Between late-night debugging and shipping tiny improvements, one constant keeps me sane: my cat, Moe. He’s equal parts roommate, study buddy, and QA engineer. If a build fails, he’ll walk across the keyboard to let me know it’s time for a break.

Moe’s personality is a perfect mix of chill and curious. He’ll sunbathe like a professional, then suddenly sprint laps to remind me that context switching is real. When I’m stuck, taking two minutes to pet him often does more than staring at a stack trace.

Moe’s contributions to the workflow

  • Quality Assurance: Paw-based integration testing (usually during meetings).
  • Break Manager: Schedules forced stretch breaks via head-bonks.
  • Focus Coach: Sits exactly where I need to read, so I refactor faster.
  • Mascot: Present for most commits; morale +10.

Things Moe loves: sun patches, small boxes that are clearly too small, and pretending the couch is a cloud. Things Moe tolerates: my camera, lint rollers, and me saying “just one more bug.”

What Moe teaches me

Slow is smooth and smooth is fast. Take breaks, keep your environment cozy, and don’t underestimate the power of a well-timed nap. Thanks, Moe.
